How the Pumping Lemma Works
01
Every regular language has a pumping length p. Any string longer than p can be split into x·y·z.
02
y is non-empty and the combined length |xy| ≤ p. y comes from the earliest repeating section.
03
For a regular language, xyⁱz must stay in the language for every i ≥ 0.
04
If pumping breaks the language for any i, the language is NOT regular.
